# Evaluation Report — 20260502_185939

**27 states** (3 inv × 3 demand × 3 cash) · **4 actions** · **5,000 train episodes** · **500 test episodes**  
Hyperparameters: `α=0.1` `γ=0.95` `ε 1.0→0.05`

---

## Overview

Q-learning and SARSA were trained on a 27-state inventory replenishment problem and evaluated against four baselines across 500 test episodes from a neutral start state. Both algorithms use identical hyperparameters.

---

## Reward Comparison

| Rank | Policy | Avg Reward | Std | CV |
|------|--------|----------:|----:|---:|
| 1 | Order-up-to | 3,035 | 1,298 | 0.43 |
| 2 | **Q-learning** | **3,026** | **1,401** | **0.46** |
| 3 | Random | 2,445 | 1,276 | 0.52 |
| 4 | SARSA | 2,323 | 1,347 | 0.58 |
| 5 | Fixed (medium) | 2,252 | 1,269 | 0.56 |
| 6 | No reorder | −1,011 | 2,750 | 2.72 |

Q-learning outperforms SARSA by **703 reward points** and produces more consistent outcomes (CV 0.46 vs 0.58). Order-up-to (3,035) is competitive but encodes domain-specific logic directly and cannot adapt to cash state.

---

## Learning Curves

Q-learning reached 80% of its final smoothed reward by episode 0; SARSA reached the same threshold at episode 2. Q-learning converged faster, consistent with off-policy learning targeting the greedy policy directly. By the final 500 training episodes, Q-learning averaged **3,026** vs SARSA at **2,323**.

Q-learning achieves a lower stockout rate than SARSA (65.4% vs 69.0%), indicating a more proactive replenishment strategy.

## Recommendation

> **Recommended model: Q-learning**
>
> Q-learning achieves the highest average reward among RL agents (3,026), a 703-point lead over SARSA, with a stockout rate of 65.4% and holding cost of 1,081. It converged to a stable policy by episode 0 and generalises sensibly across all cash states. While Order-up-to (3,035) is competitive, it is a hand-crafted heuristic that ignores cash availability. Q-learning learns this behaviour from experience and is better suited for environments where cash constraints actively shape replenishment decisions.
